Overview

The structure of the profile page was counterintuitive and difficult to navigate, especially for team members. The flawed layout led to confusion and frequent mistakes.

I recognized the need for change and led the design direction, user experience improvements, and front-end development of a new profile page. I focused on updating the flow to align with modern design standards and improve usability.

The restructuring of the page flow made it more intuitive and up to date with current best practices. The redesigned layout streamlined navigation and reduced accidental mistakes, making the profile page easier to understand and interact with.

Highlights

Separation of Profile Information and Account Details

Separate the account details to their own section to minimize the confusion of what users were editing.

Display first approach

Like standard practice I designed the profile to display information first with the option to edit to improve the overall layout and flow.

Team Member Flow

Changing the structure for the team members section to make it easier for each team member to access their information when in the profile.

05

Conclusion

The redesign of the profile page significantly improved its structure and usability. By separating account holder information and account details, we minimized confusion and made the page easier to navigate. The updated "display first" approach, with the option to edit, streamlined the flow and reduced the risk of mistakes. Additionally, the improved team member flow allowed users to access their own profile details, rather than the account holder's, enhancing their experience.

These changes have led to a more intuitive profile page, resulting in fewer errors and a smoother user experience.
